Prayer bpn12476 in Samoan

##SHORT OBLIGATORY PRAYER *TO BE RECITED ONCE IN TWENTY-FOUR HOURS, AT NOON TATALO PU’UPU’U FA’APOLOA’IGA E TASI LE FAIGA I LE LUASEFULU-FA ITULA, I LE AOAULI. Lo’u Atua e, ou te molimau atu, na faia e Lau Afio a’u ina ia iloa Oe ma tapua’i atu ia te Oe. Ou te tautino atu i lenei itula, lo’u augavale ma Lou malosi, o lo’u mativa i le ma Lou tamaoaiga. E leai lava se tasi Atua na o Oe, o le Fesoasoani i Puapugā, o le Puna– o– le–Ola.

Short Obligatory Prayer in English

#Short obligatory prayer ##To be recited once in twenty-four hours, at noon I bear witness, O my God, that Thou hast created me to know Thee and to worship Thee. I testify, at this moment, to my powerlessness and to Thy might, to my poverty and to Thy wealth. There is none other God but Thee, the Help in Peril, the Self-Subsisting. “The daily obligatory prayers are three in number.

No tin ningun otro Dios sino Bo, e auxilio den peliger, esun ku ta subsistí di Su mes. (Source category: Short Obligatory Prayer) (Bahaiprayers.net ID: 6865)
